S.A.F.E. EMDR

Somatic and Attachment focused EMDR is the same protocol as EMDR, there are a few added components however to increase attunement to physiological experiences as well as attachment experiences that can take place in the room. Deborah Kennard who trained under Francine Shapiro the founder of EMDR realized there were some ways to make the EMDR process within the room feel more comfortable, safe and mindful and the therapist more aware, relational and empathetic.

Some Added components:

The answer: an exercise to do in the beginning to explore the way the client has learned how to survive in their life.

Mindfulness exercises: explore present moment physical and emotional sensations while also exploring boundaries within the room together to create more security and understanding.  

Therapist noticing the client’s way of survival (the answer) and how it shows up in the reprocessing. 

Noticing not only what happened but also what did not happen, what was missing that was needed at the time.  

Opportunity to move, use gesture to signify new message about self. Particularly for people who have experienced physical abuse or deep physical pain, this can be a way of becoming empowered. 

There are moments where you think, “Maybe that thing that I had to go through impacts how I am now, makes me more anxious than I need to be, maybe making it difficult for me to trust/open up/ feel secure/ feel happy/and inevitably affecting the way I live my life.”

Whether one big incident of trauma or many small ones over time, what we experience and how we think about it can influence the way we interact with ourselves and with others.

EMDR can help by making some of those difficult memories and sensations less intense and less likely to impact you in the same way. Even though you will still know those memories happened, they may not carry as much power. There is a chance to create new beliefs and feelings about yourself that can move you toward living the life you are wanting to live while at the same time letting go of the messages  about yourself that are not as helpful in your current experience.
