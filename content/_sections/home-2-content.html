Welcome! I'm Lucy and I appreciate you being here!

 

 

Clients I work with often resonate with these sentiments: 

 

 

 

"I have experienced trauma in my life and it has become really hard to trust myself."

 

 

 

"I feel like I should be over things that happened but my body keeps responding as if it is still happening and these things in my past still affect me."

 

 

"I am a people-pleaser and put others' needs before my own. I have trouble making decisions, setting boundaries or knowing what I like."

 

 

 

 

Lucy Sherman, MS, NCC, CRC

She/Her

EMDR Therapist specialized in working with complex trauma and sexual trauma

 

A Space for You

We create a validating space that honors all of you while I guide you in healing from the trauma you have gone through!

 

The space we build together invites imagination, humor and openness that has been hard to access. 

 

We experiment together and create a comforting tool kit that can help you strengthen your relationship with your intuition and trust in self.

 

 

 

4 Changes that Happen for Clients in our Work Together

 

1) Trust!  They trust themselves! They notice their initial response and feel comfort in making decisions for themselves and prioritizing their needs.

 

2) Feeling connected and regulated! Yes, they still have the memories and know what happened but without that big jolt to the system every time something connected to the past comes up.The feeling of on edge, hypervigilance and tension in the body changes to feeling more grounded in the present and regulated. 

 

3) The world gets bigger! They start to not worry so much about being perfect or taking up as little space as possible. They try things, creative imaginative things, that they haven’t before and they feel they have got their own back through it.

 

4) Boundaries! They are aware of their own energy vs. others and notice what they need to support themselves in keeping boundaries that will help them. 



 

 

I have a special passion for working with Highly Sensitive, self-identified women between 20 and 35.
